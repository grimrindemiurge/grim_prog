Dev notes

  • Mainframe computer – a mainframe computer, informally called a mainframe or big iron, is a computer used primarily by large organizations for critical applications like bulk data processing for tasks such as censuses, industry and consumer statistics, enterprise resource planning, and large-scale transaction processing.

Developer acronyms

  • ISP – Internet Service Provider
  • URL – Uniform Resource Locator
  • TCP – Transmission Control Protocol
  • IP – Internet Protocol (a global addressing mechanism)
  • FTP – File Transfer Protocol
  • CDN – Content Delivery Network
  • NSFNET – National Science Foundation Network (a national “backbone” network originally connecting five supercomputing centres — at Princeton University, the University of Pittsburgh, the University of California, San Diego, the University of Illinois, and Cornell University.)
  • SNSs – social networking sites
